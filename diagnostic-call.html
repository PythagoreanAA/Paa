The Diagnostic Call

A diagnostic call is not coaching, consulting, or a pitch. It is a structural reading of a system: what is load-bearing, what is compensatory, what is missing, and what cannot persist as currently arranged.

Call format Fit test
The output is clarity. Sometimes clarity includes a direct “no.”

Format

  • Duration: 45 minutes
  • Preparation: none required
  • Material: you can bring a document, deck, website, org chart, concept map, or nothing
  • Output: a structural read + next actions (if any)
You do not need to “sell” your system. If it is real, structure will speak.

The call has one job: to identify the difference between:

  • what the system says it is
  • what the system actually does
  • what the system cannot do (as built)

If what you want is affirmation, persuasion help, or branding polish—this is the wrong service.

What You Bring

Bring any one of the following (or nothing at all):

  • a website or product
  • a deck, manifesto, or internal doc
  • an org structure / operating model
  • a set of beliefs or principles you claim to live by
  • a cultural or symbolic system you steward

Then answer three sentences (rough is fine):

  • System: what is it?
  • Break: what is failing or drifting?
  • Survive: what must endure across time?

What You Get

  • where meaning is leaking (symbolic drift)
  • where complexity is compensating for absence
  • what is structurally present vs decorative
  • what contradictions cannot coexist
  • what needs simplification, reinforcement, or removal

Sometimes the correct answer is not “build more.” Sometimes the answer is: remove the false load-bearing story, or the system collapses.

A system that cannot survive translation is not a system. It is a performance.

Fit Test

This is a fit if you want:

  • a clear structural diagnosis, not encouragement
  • the fastest route to what is actually true
  • to stop wasting time reinforcing a broken frame
  • to build a system that survives pressure

This is not a fit if you want:

  • branding polish, copywriting, or persuasion help
  • therapy, coaching, or relational processing
  • validation for a conclusion you already decided
  • a “nice conversation” with no hard edges
