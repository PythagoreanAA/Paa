Geometry
of Number

The ancients did not regard number as abstraction alone. They perceived number as visible intelligibility: proportion becoming form, relation becoming geometry, geometry becoming metaphysical instruction.

Each glyph below is a geometric condensation of principle: Monad through Ennead, and the destabilizing excess of the Undecad.

Hover over each glyph to reveal its hidden instruction. These forms are not ornamental diagrams, but thresholds: number rendered visible as law, tension, harmony, proportion, rupture, and return.
