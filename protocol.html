The Diagnostic Call Protocol

Home Organizations Personal About Book

The call is not a consultation theatre. It is a structural test. We compress the problem, run diagnostic probes, and end with a verdict that can survive contact with reality.

Inputs required (pre-call)

Bring only what can be examined. No biographies. No fog.

  • Object: what system is under diagnosis
  • Pressure: what stress is exposing failure
  • Decision: what must be decided in 30–90 days
  • Constraints: non-negotiables (time, money, politics, ethics)
  • Evidence: up to 3 artifacts (doc, deck, screenshots, job post, messaging)

What you leave with

  • A map of what is load-bearing vs decorative
  • Named contradictions that will produce collapse over time
  • Constraints that must be respected to avoid structural debt
  • One next move that holds under pressure
  • A clear “proceed / revise / decline” verdict

Call structure (25–45 minutes)

1
Compression. Reduce the situation to a stable sentence: object + pressure + decision + constraints.
2
Structural tests. Load-bearing scan, contradiction scan, incentives, feedback integrity, time horizon, role clarity.
3
Verdict. Proceed / revise / decline. You get a next move and the minimum repair required (if any).

Core questions (used on the call)

  • What does success look like in observable terms?
  • What have you tried that did not hold?
  • What must remain true for this to work?
  • Which two desires cannot coexist here?
  • Who benefits if nothing changes?
  • What signal are you ignoring because it is humiliating?
  • Does the plan hold in 6 weeks, 6 months, and 6 years?
  • Where do authority and responsibility diverge?

Verdict logic

The call ends cleanly. Not with motivation. With a recommendation.

  • Proceed: coherence is sufficient; next move is clear
  • Revise: contradictions or incentives will break the plan; repair first
  • Decline: constraint set makes the desired outcome impossible without violating reality

Guardrails

  • This is not therapy, motivation, or affirmation.
  • We do not diagnose feelings. We diagnose structure.
  • We do not end with “it depends.” We end with a verdict.
  • If the correct answer is “no,” the method says “no.”

If you want the method applied to a larger scope (team, positioning overhaul, hiring alignment), the diagnostic call determines whether that work is justified.
